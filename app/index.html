Living Light

“A lamp which harvests its energy through the photosynthetic process of the plant”

About

Climate change and the growing scarcity of raw materials symbolise a need for urgent replacement systems and redefining our relationship with nature. Nature is full of energy we are not aware of. Implementing natural processes in our systems can create a whole new sustainable energy system.

‘Living Light’, a lamp which harvests its energy by the electrons released through bacteria in the soil during the photosynthetic process of the plant. A design which shows the first step towards a future where plants will be part of our energy system.

Ermi van Oers created the Living Light during her graduation project at Willem de Kooning Academy in Rotterdam. Within her fascination for Biodesign, she discovered ‘microbial energy’: energy generated by bacteria and harvested by microbial fuel cells. She collaborated with Plant-e, a research group that is focussed on applying and developing the microbial fuel cell technology to plants.

“With this ‘living energy’ there will arise a new connection between human, nature and technology, there will grow a partnership between them. Imagine how you would take care of a lamp if it was actually alive.”

A healthy plant can produce up to 0,1 mW, which is enough to be working as a night lamp and will fall asleep with you to charge itself for the next night.

The duration of light depends on the well-being of the plant. So the better you take care of the plant, the more energy you will receive back.
